Patince Namadingo

Patience Namadingo, born on May 28, 1989, in Kapalamula Village, Balaka District, Malawi, is a renowned gospel singer and songwriter. He began his music career with the debut album Goseni in 2007, followed by Tili ndi Yesu in 2010, which featured the hit "Mtendere" and became Malawi's top-selling gospel album between 2010 and 2012. Namadingo is celebrated for his multilingual mashups and collaborations with artists like Giddes Chalamanda and Lulu. In 2020, he received an honorary doctorate in community development from the University of South Africa. Beyond music, he's known for philanthropic efforts, including fundraising for cancer patients and supporting underprivileged communities.For More Info

Eli-Njuchi

Eli Njuchi (born Chifuniro Steven Magalasi) is a Malawian singer, songwriter, and performer known for his Afro-dancehall and R&B fusion. Starting his music journey at 13, he gained prominence with hits like "Illuminati" and albums such as The Book of Z and The Book of Eli. His lyrical maturity and dynamic performances have earned him accolades, including UMP Awards for Best New Act and Male Artist of the Year. Beyond music, Eli serves as a brand ambassador for organizations like TNM and Nyasa Big Bullets FC, reflecting his influence in Malawian pop culture.For More Info

Kell-Kay

Kell Kay, born Kelvin Kambwiri on May 20, 1993, in Lilongwe, Malawi, is a celebrated Afro-R&B singer and songwriter. He gained national recognition in 2014 with his hit single "Ndilore Ndipite" and solidified his status through collaborations like "Mwano" with Martse. His 2019 album Love After 24 marked a significant milestone, launched at the Bingu International Convention Centre where he proposed to his longtime partner, Tammie. Kell Kay's soulful blend of R&B, Afrobeat, and Afropop has earned him multiple accolades, including the Nyasa Music Award for Best R&B Artist in 2017 and 2018. For More Info

Theresa

Theresa Phondo is a renowned Malawian Afro-R&B gospel artist celebrated for her emotive vocals and uplifting music. Formerly a member of Zathu Band, she gained prominence with her solo career, notably with the hit single "Blessings," which surpassed one million streams on Spotify, making her the most streamed female gospel musician from Malawi. Her collaborations with international artists like Noël Mio and Sal LY have broadened her appeal, and her music continues to inspire audiences both locally and globally. For More Info

Gwamba

Gwamba, born Duncan Zgambo on November 4, 1990, in Lilongwe, Malawi, is a prominent rapper and philanthropist. He began his music career in 2005 with the rap group Pittie Boys and transitioned to a solo artist in 2009. Initially known for secular hip-hop tracks like "Ndiyima Pachulu" and "Bola Kusache," Gwamba shifted to gospel music in 2016, releasing his debut gospel album Jesus Is My Boss. His music often addresses social issues, blending indigenous Malawian sounds with contemporary hip-hop. Beyond music, Gwamba is active in philanthropy, serving as CEO of the Shepherd Bushiri Foundation and initiating various community projects across Malawi.​For More Info

Emmie Deebo

Emmie Deebo, born Emily Zintambila, is a rising Malawian Afro-pop artist known for her vibrant fusion of Afrobeat, Amapiano, and local sounds. Hailing from Chilomoni, Blantyre, she is managed by Akometsi Entertainment and has quickly gained recognition across Southern Africa. Her 2024 EP Reflection features standout tracks like “Theka,” “Chi Mood,” and “My Baby,” showcasing her versatility. Notably, her collaboration “Hapa” with Tanzanian artist Platform amassed over 2 million views on YouTube, solidifying her regional appeal. In 2025, she was honored with the Best New Female Artist – Africa award at the Arts and Entertainment Awards in Kenya, underscoring her growing influence in the music industry. For More Info

Temwa

Temwah, born Temwa Gondwe on August 18, 1998, in Lilongwe, Malawi, is a versatile and award-winning artist known for her unique blend of Neo Soul, Amapiano, R&B, Afro Jazz, and Afro beats. Beyond her musical talents, she is a dedicated Boy Child Activist and actress. Temwah is the founder of the HE MATTERS FOUNDATION, focusing on nurturing and educating the boy child. She holds a bachelor's degree in Human Resource Management and has collaborated with notable artists like Tuksin SA, Rich Bizzy, and Lulu. Her accolades include Female Artist of the Year at the Malawi Broadcasting Corporation Entertainer of the Year Awards 2023 and the Maso Awards 2023.For More Info
